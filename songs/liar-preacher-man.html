The Story Behind This Song

Part one. This is where it all started. The origin story of the Scarlet Letter series.

Every small town has a preacher man. The one who stands at the pulpit on Sunday talking about mercy and better days — while Saturday night he's drunk again with somebody else's perfume sinking in.

She held his image up. Cooked his meals. Rocked his babies to sleep. Kept his secrets. And when the elders finally called him in to make it right — he walked out that back church door and left her standing in the war.

Then he did what liars do. He spun it. Told the world SHE was untrue. Said she strayed. Said she sinned. While she was home raising his kids. Turned her own people against her name. Branded her with that scarlet letter — the one that should have been HIS.

The liar preacher man is the reason the whole story exists. He lit the match. And every song that follows — I Didn't Die, One Night with the Devil, Smoke Me, Never Let Go, Ghost, Rodeo Cowboy — is what happened after the fire he started.

Saturday drunk. Sunday saved. God sees everything.
